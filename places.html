  • Athen

    Athens is the capital of Greece, a vast, cosmopolitan metropolis home to more than 3 million people. Ancient Athens was a powerful city-state and a significant center of science. The city is located on the Attica peninsula and is far from Hungary; by car, it’s about a 15–20 hour journey.

  • Venice

    Venice, a city built on a network of shimmering canals and marble streets, is a timeless tapestry of art, history, and romance. With its iconic gondolas gliding through narrow waterways and grand palazzos lining the Grand Canal, it feels like a living museum. From the bustling Rialto Market to the tranquil beauty of St. Mark's Square, Venice invites wandering souls to explore hidden courtyards and intimate alleys. The city’s world-class museums and celebrated architecture showcase centuries of creativity and innovation. A stroll through its winding lanes leaves you with a sense of wonder at this remarkable floating metropolis.
